Lindsey Rexroad

I used to be a teacher. I loved my job, I loved my students. However, I found that I loved using my creativity to inspire them more than I liked the content I taught. I wanted more from myself. I was telling my students to live their dreams while they were young enough to, but I wasn't following my own advice. I started to ask myself, "What are my dreams?". I knew I loved being creative. I knew I loved working with technology. I knew I loved the environment. I knew I loved to travel. What I didn't know was how to bring all of those passions together.

One day I was making 150 copies of the same worksheets and articles I had been making the last two years. I thought about all the paper I was wasting, even though I utilized the front, back, and multipage print options. I thought about my students who continuously are on their phones, and how I could easily take all my files from the computer and put it on an app for my students. They would have access to everything at home. I started looking around, I knew there had to be a way. I came across codecademy and started learning through them. I finally realized I would need a lot more intense and guided instruction, when I found Skillcrush.

Though my plans for creating an app for my students has changed, I am still driven to find creative ways to use techonolgy and coding for others.

My inspiration

Tranquility and Harmony are the two things that inspire my designs. I aspire to create designs that flow naturally and create calm for those who look at it. We all know how stressful it can be to navigate a website that you don't know much about. I aim to take the struggle away from the viewer and give them an experience that is easily navigable and a pleasant experience.

CSS/HTML

I learned to code CSS and HTML5 after I noticed the amount of paper I was using that was not necessary. I hoped to create apps for my students to use for my curriculum. I quickly learned that I loved everything about coding and it let me harness my creativity and gave me an outlet as well. I signed up for Skillcrush eight months later after trying to teach myself on my own and started learning more about the world of CSS and HTML5. As of right now, I have finished two classes and will be starting on the third, which focuses on Git, Github and the Command Line, soon.
